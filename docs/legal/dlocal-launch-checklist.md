# PlayFunded Legal and Compliance Checklist (dLocal Payouts)

This document is an operational checklist to prepare counsel review and launch.
It is not legal advice.

## 1. Product Classification (Country by Country)

Objective: confirm whether your model is treated as skill-based contest, betting, or another regulated category.

- Prepare one-page product memo:
  - User pays challenge fee.
  - Trading uses simulated bankroll.
  - Payouts are based on funded-phase performance.
  - Risk limits: drawdown, daily loss, max stake.
- Ask local counsel for each launch country to provide:
  - Required licenses (if any).
  - Required disclosures.
  - Restrictions on marketing/bonuses/affiliate promotions.
  - Tax handling obligations.

Priority countries for first legal memo:
- Ecuador
- Brazil
- Mexico
- Colombia
- Spain
- United Kingdom

## 2. Payments and Payouts Approval

Objective: avoid processor shutdown risk.

- Submit your exact business model to dLocal compliance before high volume.
- Request written confirmation of:
  - Accepted business category.
  - Permitted payout corridors and methods.
  - Any country exclusions.
- Keep fallback payout rail ready (crypto) in case a country corridor is blocked.

## 3. KYC and AML Controls

Objective: prove you only pay verified individuals and keep auditability.

- KYC required before first payout.
- Store KYC submission timestamp, reviewer, and decision reason.
- Add sanctions/PEP screening provider before scaling.
- Define suspicious activity escalation flow:
  - Freeze payout request.
  - Manual review.
  - Counsel decision.

## 4. Terms, Privacy, and Policy Updates

Objective: align legal text with real product behavior.

- Terms must explicitly state:
  - Payouts are settled in USD.
  - Payout windows and processing model.
  - Country availability and restrictions.
  - KYC requirement before payout.
- Privacy must include:
  - KYC documents and payout-related processing.
  - Data-sharing with payout processors.
- Affiliate terms must include:
  - Eligible traffic jurisdictions.
  - Prohibited claims/ads.
  - Commission clawback rules (fraud/refunds/chargebacks).

## 5. Tax and Accounting Readiness

Objective: avoid payout/tax issues after launch.

- Define whether user payouts are reported as:
  - Prizes, service payments, or other category per country.
- Determine withholding/reporting obligations per jurisdiction.
- Keep payout ledger fields complete:
  - User ID, country, amount USD, amount delivered, method, reference ID, timestamps.

## 6. Minimum Launch Gate (Do Not Skip)

Before scaling payouts, confirm all items below are complete:

- Country legal memo received for first launch markets.
- dLocal written approval for business model and payout corridors.
- KYC process tested end to end.
- Payout reconciliation runbook documented.
- Incident owner assigned for processor freezes/holds.

## 7. Counsel Packet (Send This)

Provide counsel these materials in one folder:

- Product flow (signup -> challenge -> funded -> payout).
- Current Terms and Privacy text.
- Sample user-facing payout copy in all languages.
- Processor list (dLocal + crypto rails).
- Country launch list and marketing plan.

Ask counsel to answer:

1. Is this model legal in each target country?
2. What licensing/registration is mandatory before accepting fees?
3. What wording must appear in Terms and checkout?
4. What restrictions apply to affiliate marketing claims?
5. What data retention period is required for KYC and payout records?
